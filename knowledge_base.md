## Cancellation Policy

- **Flights:** Cancellations made 30+ days before departure are refunded in full,
  minus a $50 processing fee. Cancellations made 7-10 days before departure are
  refunded 50% of the ticket price. Cancellations made less than 7 days before
  departure are non-refundable.
- **Hotels:** Most bookings can be cancelled free of charge up to 48 hours before
  check-in. Non-refundable rate bookings are excluded and are charged in full at
  time of booking.
- **Packages (flight + hotel bundles):** Cancellation terms follow the stricter of
  the two individual policies above. A single $75 bundle cancellation fee applies
  in addition to any per-component fees.
- **How to cancel:** Cancellations can be requested through the booking
  confirmation email link or by contacting support with your booking reference.
  Refunds are processed within 5-10 business days to the original payment method.
- **Airline/hotel-initiated changes:** If a flight is cancelled or a schedule is
  changed by more than 3 hours by the airline, travelers are entitled to a full
  refund or a free rebooking on the next available option.

## Baggage Policy

- **Carry-on allowance:** One carry-on bag (max 7-10 kg, size varies by airline,
  typically up to 56 x 36 x 23 cm) plus one personal item (backpack, purse, or
  laptop bag) per passenger, free of charge.
- **Checked baggage allowance:** Economy fares typically include 1 checked bag up
  to 23 kg. Premium and business fares typically include 2 checked bags up to
  32 kg each. Allowances vary by airline and route — always confirm with the
  operating carrier.
- **Excess and overweight baggage:** Additional bags or bags exceeding the weight
  limit incur airline-specific fees, generally $50-150 per bag/segment. Fees are
  higher when paid at the airport versus pre-purchased online.
- **Restricted and prohibited items:** Standard aviation security rules apply
  (liquids over 100ml in carry-on, no sharp objects, lithium batteries only in
  carry-on, etc.). Always check the departure country's aviation authority
  guidance before packing.
- **Lost, delayed, or damaged baggage:** Report to the airline's baggage desk
  before leaving the airport. Most airlines offer interim essentials
  reimbursement for delayed bags and require damage claims to be filed within
  7 days of the flight.

## Travel Insurance Policy

- **What's typically covered:** Trip cancellation or interruption, emergency
  medical treatment abroad, medical evacuation, delayed or lost baggage, and
  trip delay reimbursement (meals/accommodation after a defined delay
  threshold, e.g. 6+ hours).
- **What's typically excluded:** Pre-existing medical conditions (unless a
  waiver is purchased within a set window of initial trip deposit), extreme
  sports and adventure activities (unless an add-on rider is purchased),
  travel to destinations under a government travel advisory issued before
  booking, and cancellations for reasons not listed in the policy's covered
  reasons list.
- **When to buy:** Insurance is recommended at the time of booking to unlock
  the earliest coverage window and any pre-existing-condition waiver
  eligibility.
- **Making a claim:** Keep all receipts, medical reports, and airline
  correspondence. Claims are typically filed online within 30-90 days of the
  covered event, depending on the provider.
- **Recommended coverage level:** For international trips, a minimum of
  $50,000 in emergency medical coverage and $500-1000 in baggage/delay
  coverage is a reasonable baseline — adjust upward for adventure activities,
  older travelers, or trips to countries with high medical costs.
